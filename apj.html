APJ Abdul Kalam

Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam (1931–2015), known as the “Missile Man of India,” was one of the most inspiring scientists and leaders in Indian history. Born into a humble family in Rameswaram, he rose through dedication and hard work to become a key figure in India’s space and missile programs. He played a major role in the development of the Agni and Prithvi missiles and contributed significantly to ISRO’s satellite launch vehicle projects.

Kalam later became the 11th President of India, where he was admired for his simplicity, wisdom, and deep love for students. He believed that education, creativity, and courage were the keys to building a strong nation. Even after his presidency, he continued traveling across India, motivating millions of young people to dream big and work hard. His life remains a symbol of scientific excellence and inspirational leadership.
