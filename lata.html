Lata Mangeshkar

Lata Mangeshkar (1929–2022), often called the “Nightingale of India,” was one of the most iconic and influential playback singers in the world. With a career spanning over seven decades, she recorded more than 25,000 songs in over 30 languages. Her voice became the soundtrack of Indian cinema, touching the hearts of millions across generations.

She worked with legendary composers like SD Burman, RD Burman, Madan Mohan, and AR Rahman. Her songs carried deep emotion, purity, and grace, making her a symbol of Indian music globally. Lata Mangeshkar received countless awards, including the Bharat Ratna, India’s highest civilian honor. Her legacy continues to inspire singers and music lovers around the world.
